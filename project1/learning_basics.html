How to Play

Passing (Bumping)

Passing is a fundamental skill in volleyball, especially for receiving serves and passing the ball to a teammate for a set or attack.

Overview:

  • Positioning: Position yourself in a balanced stance with your feet shoulder-width apar. Bend your kness slightly to get ready to move.
  • Hand Placement: Form a platform with your forearms by clasping your hands together, with your thumbs parallel to each other and your arms straight out in front of you, creating a flat surface for the ball to rebound off.
  • Contact Path: Try to position yourself in the middle of the ball path so the ball meets the forearms between your wrists and elbows.
  • Contact Point: Make contact with the ball and extend your arms a little in the direction you want the ball to travel.
  • Footwork: While bumping, focus on your footwork to maintain balance and stability.

Setting

Setting is a fundamental skill that involves accurately directing the ball to a teammate for an attack.

Overview:

  • Positioning: Start by positioning yourself (or your forehead) underneath the ball, with your feet shoulder-width apart and knees slightly bent. Face your target and square shoulders to ensure accuracy.
  • Hand Placement: Raise both hands above your head, forming a triangular shape with your thumbs and index fingers. Keep fingers spread apart to create a large surface area for contacting the ball.
  • Contact Path: Position yourself to make contact with it at its highest point. Aim to contact the ball with fingertips slightly above forehead to achieve maximum control and accuracy.
  • Contact Point: Use your fingertips to cushion the ball upon contact, giving it a soft touch to set it up for your teammate. Avoid too much force because it can result in an inaccurate and unpredictable set. After making contact, continue your arm motion in teh direction of your target to ensure a smooth and fluid set.

Hitting (Spiking, Attacking)

Hitting, also known as spiking or attacking, is the act of forcefully striking the ball over the net into the opponent's court.

Overview:

  • Approach: Start your approach by taking three steps: a left-right-left sequence for right-handed hitters and the opposite for left-handed hitters. Steps should be explosive and quick, with the last step being longer and more powerful.
  • Timing: Time your approach so that you reach the peak of your jump just as the setter releases the ball.
  • Jump: Jump explosively off both feet, driving your arms to gain height. Aim to jump vertically as much as possible to maintain control and accuracy.
  • Arm Swing: Bring your hitting arm back behind your head to generate power. Swing arm forward forcefully and snap wrist at the point of contact with the ball.
  • Arm Swing: Contact the ball with the palm of your hand, using your fingertips to control the direction and trajectory of your hit.

Blocking

Blocking is a fundamental defensive skill where players jump to intercept the opponent's attack at the net, deflecting it back to their court.

Overview:

  • Positioning: Stand at the net with your hands ready to jump vertically to block the opponent's attack. Position yourself in front of the hitter to close the blocking angle.
  • Jump Timing: Time your jump with the hitter's attack, anticipate the trajectory of the ball and jump to reach the highest point of the block.
  • Positioning: Extend both arms above the net and press your hands over the net to block the ball. Keep hands wide apart to cover more space and increase chance of blocking the attack.
  • Closing the Block: Close the block by bringing your arms together as the hitter makes contact with the ball, sealing the block and minimizing gaps.
  • Footwork: Use quick footwork to adjust position and angle for blocking. Video below elaborates more on this.

Serving

Serving is fundamental because it is how a rally begins. Players use various techniques such as float serves, jump serves, topspin serves and underhand serves to initiate play.

Basic Serve Overview:

  • Stance: Start with a balanced stance behind the end line, with your feet shoulder-width apart. Position yourself slightly to the side of the court with your non-dominant foot facing slightly forward.
  • Grip: Place your palm of your non-dominant hand underneath the ball and grip it with your fingertips.
  • Toss: Toss the ball with your non-dominant hand in front of you and slightly upward, aiming for a consistent toss height. The toss should be located in line with your hitting shoulder and slightly in front of your body.
  • Contact: Swing your dominant hand upward and forward in a smooth and controlled motion, making contact with the lower half of the ball. Strike the ball with the heel of your hand or the base of your palm to generate power and direction.
  • Follow Through: After making contact with the ball, continue the motion of your serving arm upward and forward to ensure power and accuracy.

Basic Rules

These are some basic rules that are in all types of volleyball:

  • 3 touches per side: Maximum of 3 consecutive hits per side is allowed (a block would not count as a touch)
  • Teams: Each team consists of 2-6 players (usually 2 for beach vb and 6 for indoor vb)
  • Scores: A set is won by the team that first reaches 25 points (21 for beach vb) with at least a 2 point advantage.
  • Ball In/Out: Rotate clockwise each time they win the serve from the opponent. Players must rotational order throughout the set. (beach vb only rotates serves, not position)
  • Faults
  • Double touches: A player can not hit the ball twice in a row whenever it comes to their side.
  • Net Play: Players cannot touch the net during play. Players can reach over the net to block or follow through on a spike, but cannot interfere with the opponent's play.
